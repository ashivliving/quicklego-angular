No wonder, Indian marriages or weddings as they are popularly known as, are epitome of extravaganza, opulence, class and luxury. Indian marriage, mostly, are celebrated uniquely with ancient traditions and rituals, poised with the priest’s guidance and witnessed & rejoiced by family and friends. Since, these marriages (weddings) embrace such time-honored rituals and practices, marriages (weddings) especially in India, are considered as a knot of souls where love, bonding and communion are manifested ceremonially.


True, such rituals and customs have their own importance, but today it is very necessary to make the marriage legal. So, registration of marriage is very important and necessary. Given the fact that the society is changing every day and posing different kinds of challenges, complexities and difficulties in people’s life, registration of Marriage can prove to be a panacea for most of the legal hassles.


Nonetheless, Marriage registration is very essential for obtaining an established status of legality for the couple for being in a matrimonial relationship. A marriage certificate does not mean just 'another license' that one must obtain. It upholds the sanctity and legality of a marriage. It is in fact a very essential document that couples generally tend to overlook in the euphoria and fanfare of their marriage.


However, most of the couples perceive it as that 'it is a waste of time, who needs a marriage certificate? etc.' But, the truth is that such a legal authorization for marriage is indispensable for the couple. As a matter of rule, marriage registration is now compulsory. As per the Honorable Supreme Court directive dated 14.2.2006, it is mandatory for the couples to register their marriages, no matter which religion, caste or creed they belong to. And if the marriage is not registered, this can be seen as a crime and is a punishable offence.


Interestingly, most of the couples often wonder as to how to apply for marriage certificate or how to register their marriage, most of them have to scout for a lawyer/agent to help them register their marriage, most of them could barely make time from their busy schedule to take the hassle of visiting offices for information and getting the marriage registered.

  Advantages  

Supreme Court Directive Compulsory Registration

The Hon’ble Supreme Court has made it mandatory for every Indians irrespective to their religion, caste and creed to obtain a marriage certificate and it is compulsory for all the newlyweds to apply for the same within 60 days of solemnization of their marriage.

Proof of Marriage

Marriage Certificate is the acceptable legal proof of a marriage recognized by the Courts in India and Abroad. It provides protection against false denial of marriage by either of the spouses.

Visa Application

Any application for spouse visa based on employment mandatorily requires a Marriage Certificate.

Bank Deposits / Life Insurance / Mutual Funds Claims

Marriage Certificate would now be compulsorily required to be presented, while claiming of Bank Deposits, Life Insurance Proceeds, Mutual Funds etc. in case of the death of the spouse. Also, required for inducting your partner/spouse as a legal nominee in various insurance related policies.

Family Disputes

Marriage Certificate is a prima facie proof of marriage and clarifies doubts on date and validity of the marriage in unforeseen and unhappy circumstances of disputes (whether civil or criminal) between the spouses relating to divorce, dowry, maintenance and other matrimonial related cases.

Change of Spouse Name to Passport

Any addition of Spouse (Husband / Wife) name to Passport now requires a mandatory production of Marriage Certificate.

Property Proceedings/ Probation of Will

Marriage Certificate is required to file your claims to the wealth and property of your deceased spouse. It is also required to complete the probation of their will executed by them before their death.

NRI Marriages

Marriage Certificate is a vital piece of evidence/proof against false denial of marriage, abandonment by husband and cruel and ill treatment in foreign land. It provides adequate grounds to proceed legally against husbands who was already married or who later got married to someone else after marrying you.

Eligibility for Same Day Registration

  1. Both Bride & Groom must be HINDUS
  2. Marriage must have been solemnized between both of them as per Hindu rites and customs
  3. Both Bride & Groom should not be less than 21 years of age

Document Required

  1. Form of Application - A & B for Registration of Marriage under Hindu Act, 1955 with Court Fees of Rs.4/- duly signed by both Husband and Wife.
  2. Joint Affidavit by Bride (Wife) + Groom (Husband) of Notary/Executive Magistrate that the couple is married under Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 and are staying together.
  3. Affidavit of Wife/Bride’s Father/Mother with the form.
  4. Affidavit of Husband/Groom’s Father/Mother with the form.
  5. Two passport size photographs of both the parties
  6. Joint photograph of marriage of both Wife/Bride and Husband/Groom [preferably the one with Hand-knot (Hatha-ganthee)].
  7. Invitation Cards of marriage of both sides - Bride and Groom.
  8. If marriage was solemnized in a religious place, a certificate from the priest is required who solemnized the marriage.
  9. Documentary evidence of date of birth of parties (Matriculation Certificate / Passport / Birth Certificate) -Both sides (Bride and Groom).
  10. ID-Proof (Aadhaar Card/Driving License/Voter ID Card) of both Bride and Groom and their respective parents/guardian
  11. Proof of Residence of Bride/Groom of the local area comprising of Patta/ROR of the House/Property & BMC Holding Tax Receipt for the financial year in which marriage was solemnized.
  12. Treasury Challan of Rs. 16/- (if application for registration of marriage is made within one month of solemnization of marriage) or Rs. 42/- (if application for registration of marriage has been made on expiry of one month of solemnization of marriage).
  13. Attested copy of divorce decree/order in case of a divorcee and death certificate of spouse in case of widow/widower.
  14. In case one of the parties belong to other than Hindu, Buddhist, Jain and Sikh religions, a conversion certificate from the priest who solemnized the marriage(in case of Hindu Marriage Act.

Timings (Only on working days)

  1. WednesDay - 3P.M. to 5 P.M.
  2. SaturDay - 12P.M. to 2 P.M.

Eligibility for Marriage Registration (Already Celebrated Marriage)

  1. Both Bride & Groom must be 21 years of age and above.
  2. There is no bar on the religion of the Bride & Groom.

Document Required

  1. Form of Application – Form V u/s 15 of the Special Marriage Act, 1954 for Application for Registration of Marriage.
  2. Joint Affidavit by Bride + Groom of Notary/Executive Magistrate that the marriage has been solemnized between both of them, are staying together, do not have more than one spouse living on the time of making the application or registration, have completed the age of 21 years, are within the degree of prohibited relationship of marriage.
  3. Affidavit of Bride in case of change in surname.
  4. Joint photograph (Postcard size) of marriage of both Bride and Groom [preferably the one with Hand-knot (Hatha-ganthee)].
  5. Invitation Cards of marriage of both sides - Bride and Groom.
  6. Proof of Date of Birth (Matriculation Certificate, Birth Certificate) -Both sides (Bride and Groom).
  7. ID-Proof (Aadhaar Card/Driving License/Voter ID Card) of both Bride and Groom and their respective parents/guardian.
  8. Proof of Residence (Local Area) of Husband(Bride-Groom)/Wife(Bride) – Rent Agreement (Min. 6 months of stay before applying) and Electricity Bill (Latest) or Patta/ROR, Holding Tax Receipt(Current Financial Year) and Electricity Bill (Latest).
  9. 3 Nos. of Witness along with their ID Proof.

Timings (Only on working days)

  1. WednesDay - 3P.M. to 5 P.M.
  2. SaturDay - 12P.M. to 2 P.M.

Eligibility for Court Marriage

  1. Applicable to unmarried/divorced couples.
  2. Age limit- Bride- 18, Bride Groom – 21.

Document Required

  1. Form of Application – Notice of Intending Marriage u/s 5 of the Special Marriage Act, 1954.
  2. Passport size photograph of Both Parties (3 Nos.).
  3. Affidavit of Bride in case of change in surname.
  4. Proof of Date of Birth (Matriculation Certificate, Birth Certificate) -Both sides (Bride and Groom).
  5. ID-Proof (Aadhaar Card/Driving License/Voter ID Card) of both Bride and Groom and their respective parents/guardian
  6. Proof of Residence (Local Area) of Husband(Bride-Groom)/Wife(Bride) – Rent Agreement (Min. 6 months of stay before applying) and Electricity Bill (Latest) or Patta/ROR, Holding Tax Receipt(Current Financial Year) and Electricity Bill (Latest).
  7. 3 Nos. of Witness along with their ID Proof.
